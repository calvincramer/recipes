## Ingredients
- white carrot, peeled, cut 45 degrees around 1/4 inch then julienned into sticks
    - can sub with napa cabbage
- fish cake pre-made or scratch, cut into strips
    - optionally Dace fish cake
- pork, marinated with corn flour and oil, sliced thinly
    - sub chicken and ginger
- Chinese dry sausage, cut at angle
- green onion, slice medium
- cilantro, chopped medium
- glutinous rice flour for balls, 300g for 5 servings
- white pepper
- optionally ginger, julienned
- optionally red hot pepper for heat
- optionally small dried shrimp to flavor stock

## Steps
1. In a wok, bring water to boil
2. Make tang yuan ball dough by adding glutinous rice flour to bowl, add cold water and mix until pretty thick dough. Don't overwork. Make balls right before using. The dough shouldn't be wet. (Don't make into balls yet)
3. boil white carrot until mostly soft, still some time left to finish
4. Once carrots are (almost) done take them out and put aside, reserve water for later (if only using one wok)
5. In empty wok or separate pan, saute Chinese sausage, fish cake, then pork in a little oil. Add a little salt
6. Optionally add some julienned ginger
7. Add in white carrots (meat is not done yet)
8. Cook a few minutes
9. Add in reserved water
10. Bring to boil
11. Form and add in tang yuan balls. Make sure balls don't stick. Don't stir when putting balls in for a minute or so.
12. Cook until balls float
13. Salt to taste
14. Add some green onion, cilantro, white pepper. 
15. Serve
16. Have green onion, cilantro, white pepper available for people to add

## Fish Cake from Scratch
Ingredients
- fish paste or fish fillet blend
    - 100% lianggui 凌鱼 fish, or 30% shrimp and 70% any white fish like dace
- cilantro
- green onion
- salt

Steps
1. mix everything
2. form into disks / pucks
3. fry
4. cool (so don't fall apart when cut)
